Welcome

I'm a lecturer in biostatistics in the CHICAS research group at Lancaster university. I'm interested in statistical machine learning and how it can be applied in biosciences and epidemiology. I hold a fellowship from the MRC in biostatistics.

I'm a visiting researcher at Manchester University with Magnus Rattray, where I help work on BitSeq, a method for quantifying gene and isoform eqpression from RNA-seq data.

I helped create GPy, a python package for developing Gaussian process models. I also created GPClust, a package for clustering time series models using GPs, Dirichlet processes and variational Bayes.

Previously I worked at Sheffield University with Neil Lawrence after completing a PhD with Keith Worden.
